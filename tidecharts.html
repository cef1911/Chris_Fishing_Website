Fishing Tide Strategy

It is a good strategy to fish from shore 2 hours before and after high tide. This is because the moving tides cause the smaller baitfish to migrate and be active. With the activity of the smaller baitfish, the larger predatory fish feed on them. I have caught predatory fish like bluefish, striped bass, seabass, blackfish, and porgies on both the incoming and outgoing tides.

I have also caught striped bass and bluefish on low tides. This is because with the smaller amount of water available during a low tide the predatory fish can ambush the smaller baitfish easily. The smaller baitfish don't have a larger area of water to hide from the predatory fish.
